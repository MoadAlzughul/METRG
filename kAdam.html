Khaled Adam Over ten years, Khaled Adam has been working on optimizing structure evolution during the thermo-mechanical process. Recently, Khaled has held assistant professor at Kennesaw State University and his main research interest is in the area of modeling the microstructure evolution by using viscoplasticity Fast Fourier Transformer (VPFFT) and Monte Carlo Potts model codes. Khaled has a rich experience in using Electron Backscattered diffraction (EBSD) through Scan electron microscope (SEM) as well as Transmission electron microscope (TEM). Moreover, Khaled is expert in using CAE software (SolidWorks), and finite element software such as ANSYS, Digmat, Abaqus. Currently Khaled is focusing on developing novel heterogeneous microstructures using computational techniques, so as to achieve an optimized strength-ductility synergy in commercial metallic alloys. Khaled has earned his BSc and MSc in mechanical engineering from University of Benghazi (UOB) the most prestigious university in Libya. He has worked as a teaching assistant and lecturer at mechanical engineering department, UOB from 2004 to 2008. He received his Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering, from the Washington State University in 2015. During his Ph.D. study Khaled has word as a researcher and teaching assistant, Afterward, Khaled has worked as a Post doc researcher at Washington State University. Khaled is married to Reima Aboabker and has two children, Derar and Eyad. At present time, Khaled is coaching soccer to Savannah United for age (U6 and U12) boys.
